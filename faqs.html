General Questions

What is a central vacuum system?

A central vacuum system is a powerful and efficient vacuum cleaner that is installed in a home or building. It consists of a main unit that is typically installed in a basement or garage, and a network of pipes that runs through the walls to inlets located throughout the building.

How does a central vacuum system work?

The central vacuum system works by using a motor to create suction, which is then transferred through a network of pipes to a central unit. The dirt and debris are collected in a canister or bag located in the central unit, which can be easily emptied when full.

What are the benefits of a central vacuum system?

Central vacuum systems offer several benefits, including more powerful suction, quieter operation compared to portable vacuums, improved indoor air quality, and convenience with built-in inlets in various rooms.


Installation and Maintenance

Can I install a central vacuum system myself?

While it is possible to install a central vacuum system yourself, it is recommended to hire a professional to ensure proper installation and avoid potential issues.

How often should I maintain my central vacuum system?

Regular maintenance is important for optimal performance. It is recommended to check the filters and bags every 6 to 12 months and to have the system inspected by a professional annually.

What type of maintenance is required?

Maintenance typically involves emptying the canister or replacing the bag, cleaning or replacing filters, checking for clogs in the pipes, and ensuring that the motor and other components are functioning correctly.
